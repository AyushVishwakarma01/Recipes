Fish

Many species of fish are consumed as food in virtually all regions around the world. Fish has been an important source of protein and other nutrients for humans throughout history.

English does not have a special culinary name for food prepared from these animals, as it does with pig vs. pork, and as does Spanish pescado vs. pez. In culinary and fishery contexts, fish may include shellfish, such as molluscs, crustaceans and echinoderms; more expansively, seafood covers both fish and other marine life used as food.

instructions

step 1-Ingredients

A. 3 red finger chillies, de-seeded and roughly chopped

B. 1 tablespoon ground coriander

C.1 teaspoon ground cumin

D.1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

E.2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped

F.3cm piece of fresh root ginger, grated

G.1 tablespoon tamarind paste

H.400g MSC certified pollock fillet, skinless and boneless

I.Salt and freshly ground black pepper- to taste

J.50ml sunflower oil

K.1/2 medium onion, sliced

L.2 green or red chillies, split open

M.1 tomato, chopped

N.125ml canned coconut milk

O.1 tablespoon fresh coriander, chopped

Step 2-frying

1. To make a curry paste, blend together the 3 red finger chillies, 1/2 of the ground coriander, the cumin, turmeric, garlic, ginger and tamarind paste until smooth, using either a food processor or pestle and mortar.

2. Cut the pollock fillets into halves (or chunky pieces if really large), then season well with salt and pepper.

3. Heat the oil to medium/high in a deep frying pan, add the onion and fry for about 5 minutes, until golden brown. Then add the split chillies, curry paste, the other 1/2 of the ground coriander and a little more oil if necessary, slowly stirring everything together.

4. Mix in the tomato and fry for 1 minute, then add the coconut milk and season with more salt and pepper before reducing the heat.

5. Add the fish to the pan, cover and simmer gently over a medium heat for about 5-6 minutes, turning the fish over half-way through. Add the fresh coriander and season again with salt and pepper.

6. Serve in bowls with boiled rice and naan bread as sides.

7.Enjoy with Roti
